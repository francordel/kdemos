What is Doodle?

Doodle is a meeting scheduling tool founded in Switzerland in 2007. It lets organizers create availability polls where participants mark which dates or time slots work for them. It's widely used in corporate settings and integrates with Google Calendar and Outlook.

The downsides: the free plan shows ads, limits active polls, and requires the organizer to create an account. For a casual group meetup or a small team meeting, it's overkill.

How to use KDEMOS instead of Doodle

  1. Go to kdemos.com, type your name and click "Create calendar".

  2. Pick the proposed dates on the calendar and copy the generated link.

  3. Share the link in your group chat (WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, email…).

  4. Each person opens the link, writes their name and marks their available days.

  5. The calendar shows in real time which day works best for the most people.

When to use Doodle instead of KDEMOS

Doodle may be the better choice if you need time-slot polls (not just day polls), integration with corporate calendars, or if your company already pays for Doodle Premium. For everything else, KDEMOS covers 90% of use cases with zero cost and zero registration.

Other Doodle alternatives

Besides KDEMOS, other tools in this space include When2meet, Rallly, Lettucemeet and NeedToMeet. Each has its strengths, but KDEMOS stands out for its simplicity and mobile experience.
