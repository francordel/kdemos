What is Rallly?

Rallly (with three L's, rallly.co) is an open source scheduling tool inspired by Doodle. Participants vote on dates or time slots, and the organizer finalizes the winning option. It's self-hostable, well-designed and has a growing community, one of the best Doodle alternatives out there.

The friction point: to create and manage polls you need to register as organizer, and the free hosted plan keeps some features for the Pro tier.

Why pick KDEMOS over Rallly?

Zero accounts, even for the organizer

On KDEMOS nobody registers: type your name, pick the dates, share the link. For casual groups, friends, families, five-a-side teams, this doubles response rates.

Built for group chats

One-click share buttons for WhatsApp and Telegram, and a link that unfurls with a proper preview. The whole flow assumes your group lives in a chat app, because it does.

Live results

Votes appear in real time while the group is discussing, no refresh needed. It turns the poll into something people watch together.

When Rallly is the better choice

If you need hour-level time slots ("Tuesday at 3pm or 5pm?"), the built-in finalize step, or want to self-host the tool on your own server, Rallly is excellent and we happily recommend it. For pure "which day works for everyone?" questions, KDEMOS is faster.

Get started in 30 seconds

  1. Open kdemos.com and type your name, no account.

  2. Select the candidate days and copy your unique link.

  3. Share it in your group chat; everyone marks their days and the best date pops out in real time.
